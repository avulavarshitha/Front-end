FACTS ABOUT APJ ABDUL KALAM

  • Humble Beginnings: Abdul Kalam was born into a Tamil Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. His father was a boat owner, and his mother was a housewife. Despite his modest background, Kalam went on to become one of India's most renowned scientists.
  • Passion for Learning: Kalam was a voracious reader and had a deep love for education from a young age. He worked as a newspaper vendor to support his education during his school years.
  • First Major Project: Kalam started his career at the Aeronautical Development Establishment (ADE) of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and was involved in designing a small hovercraft, which was his first major project.
  • Key Architect of India’s Missile Program: Often called the "Missile Man of India," Kalam was instrumental in the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles, which bolstered India's defense capabilities.
  • Integral Role in India’s Nuclear Program: Kalam played a significant role in the successful nuclear tests conducted at Pokhran in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power on the global stage.
  • Scientific Leadership: Before becoming President, Kalam served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • People’s President: Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India in 2002. He was widely loved by the Indian public and earned the nickname "People’s President" for his accessibility and humility.
  • Focus on Youth and Vision for India: Kalam was deeply committed to education and youth empowerment. He often interacted with students and emphasized the importance of dreaming big. His vision for India was encapsulated in his book "India 2020," which aimed to make India a developed nation by 2020.
  • Prolific Writer: Kalam authored several inspirational books, including "Wings of Fire," "Ignited Minds," and "The Luminous Sparks." His writings continue to inspire millions of people across the world.
  • Final Moments: Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He collapsed due to a cardiac arrest, leaving behind a legacy of inspiration, dedication, and service to the nation.
